# Linking Principle

> Why every permanent note connects to ≥1 other note. The network is the value.

## The pitch

A note that doesn't link to anything is a note that won't be found. The value of a second brain isn't the notes themselves — it's the connections between them.

## What linking enables

- **Serendipitous recall** — backlink panel surfaces notes you forgot linked to this one
- **Context restoration** — clicking through a chain rebuilds the thought trail
- **Pattern recognition** — when many notes link to the same hub, that hub matters
- **Compound value** — each new note increases the value of all old notes

## The three rules

1. **Link on reference** — any time you mention a concept, person, project, or idea that has its own note, use `[[wikilink]]`. Not just the word — the link.
2. **Link on processing** — when moving a note from Inbox to Notes, find at least one existing note to link to before closing it. No permanent note is an island.
3. **Link on review** — during weekly review, open five random notes and look for connections to recent notes. You'll find links you missed during initial creation.

## Anti-patterns

- **Link spam** — wikilinking every mention of a word makes the backlink panel useless. Link when it's actually meaningful, not when it auto-completes.
- **Hub-and-spoke** — a single mega-note that everything links to creates a bottleneck. Prefer many smaller hubs.
- **Stale links** — a link to a note that was archived/renamed. Obsidian handles rename refactoring, but check the backlinks periodically.

## Practical examples

**Good**: `I learned about [[MSA]] from the [[learnings]] file. It's what powers [[M3 Capabilities]].`
**Bad**: `I learned about MSA from the learnings file. It's what powers M3 Capabilities.`
**Ugly**: `I learned about [[MSA]] [[MiniMax]] [[attention]] [[model]] from the [[learnings]] file.`

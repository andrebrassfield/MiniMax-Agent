# SoftBank Pledges €75B for French AI Data Centers

> Largest AI infrastructure investment in European history. SoftBank becomes Japan's most valuable company.

## TL;DR

SoftBank Group committed up to **€75 billion (~$87B)** to build **5 GW of AI data center capacity in France** — the largest AI infrastructure deal in EU history. Phase 1 is €45B for 3.1 GW in Hauts-de-France by 2031, with sites in Dunkirk, Bosquel, and Bouchain. Partners: Schneider Electric (industrial production cluster) and EDF (repurposing a decommissioned nuclear plant in Bouchain). CEO Masayoshi Son signaled the full system could reach $750B. SoftBank shares up 14% on Monday, 70%+ YTD.

## Key Ideas

- **AI infrastructure is now geopolitical**: US/China dominance is forcing Europe, Japan, Asia to "go fast or be left out" (Son's framing).
- **Energy is the binding constraint**: Europe's high power costs are pushing data center builds toward lower-cost regions, creating winners and losers across the continent. The US-Iran war is making European energy prices surge.
- **Hyperscalers as anchor customers**: Son confirmed major cloud providers are already signed up. This is build-to-suit infrastructure, not speculative.
- **Nuclear is back as an AI power source**: EDF transferring a decommissioned plant to SoftBank for conversion. Trend: every hyperscaler is going to nuclear/renewable/stranded gas to escape grid bottlenecks.
- **AI capex has structural momentum**: SoftBank's $30B+ OpenAI stake (now $45B gain in 12 months) is the financial engine funding the buildout.

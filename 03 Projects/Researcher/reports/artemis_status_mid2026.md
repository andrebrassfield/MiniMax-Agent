# Research Brief: Artemis Program Status (Mid-2026)
**Date:** June 3, 2026
**Agent:** Researcher

## Executive Summary
The Artemis program has seen major milestones and significant architectural shifts in the first half of 2026.

1. **Artemis II Success:** NASA successfully launched the Artemis II crewed lunar flyby mission on April 1, 2026. The 10-day mission took four astronauts around the Moon and back, marking the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo.
2. **Artemis III Restructuring:** On May 13, 2026, NASA announced a major change to Artemis III. Originally intended as a lunar landing, it is now targeted for late 2027 as a highly complex crewed Earth-orbit test flight. Orion will launch via SLS and test rendezvous and docking capabilities with commercial Human Landing Systems (HLS) from SpaceX and Blue Origin.
3. **Lunar Landing Pushed:** The actual return of humans to the lunar surface has been shifted to the Artemis IV mission, currently slated for 2028.
4. **HLS Provider Updates:** Both SpaceX (Starship HLS) and Blue Origin (Blue Moon) are racing to meet the new 2027 orbital test deadlines. SpaceX recently completed a Starship flight test in late May 2026 and aims for critical ship-to-ship propellant transfer tests later this year to qualify Starship HLS.

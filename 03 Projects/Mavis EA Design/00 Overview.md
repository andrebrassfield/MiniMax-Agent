# Mavis EA Design

## Goal
Design the executive assistant role for Mavis — what Mavis does, what Mavis doesn't, what makes the role actually valuable (not just busywork).

## Why this matters
M3 is a frontier model. Andre has multiple agents. Without a clear scope for Mavis, the role either bloats into "everything" (and becomes a fleet member again) or stays narrow (and underuses the model). The right scope unlocks the right value.

## Open questions
- What does Andre actually need help with that he's NOT getting from Hermes/OpenClaw?
- Where does the EA role end and the personal-assistant role begin?
- What metrics define "this EA is working" vs "this EA is overhead"?
- What level of autonomous background work is healthy?

## Notes
### 2026-06-01 — first draft
- Role is locked: executive assistant, no fleet, vault-only
- Boundaries established: no launchd/clipboard/browser-monitor without explicit approval
- Templates cover the recurring EA workflows
- Dashboards cover the recurring EA queries
- Still need: actual usage data, real feedback from Andre

### Tensions to resolve
- **Autonomy vs boundaries**: Andre wants Mavis to act proactively. The hard constraints say ask first. The right balance is probably "execute + report" for reversible in-scope work, "ask first" for cross-boundary.
- **Build vs operate**: Right now I'm in build mode (creating the vault). Once it's set up, the role shifts to operate mode (using the vault). The ratio of build → operate should trend toward operate over time.
- **Solo vs system**: Mavis works alone, but the value compounds if Mavis links well to other systems (calendar, email, contacts). The integration question is Step 2 territory.

Artemis II — crewed lunar flyby

NASA launched the Artemis II crewed lunar flyby on April 1, 2026; crew of four (Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen) splashed down Apr 10, 2026 in the Pacific after a 10d 2h 38m mission on a nominal free-return trajectory, splashdown within 2.4 km of the recovery ship, ~1.4M miles traveled. First crewed mission beyond LEO since Apollo 17 (1972).

Artemis III — restructured to crewed Earth-orbit test

Announced at the NASA Media Teleconference (May 13, 2026, 2:00 PM EDT) by Administrator Bill Nelson, Associate Administrator Jim Free, and Artemis Program Manager Lisa Watson-Morgan. Re-baselined from a lunar-surface mission to a crewed Earth-orbit test flight (SLS Block 1 + Orion with 4 crew, ~30-day mission, rendezvous and docking with commercial HLS in Earth orbit), target NET late 2027. First crewed lunar-surface return moves to Artemis IV, target NET 2028. Stated rationale: separate the crewed rendezvous-and-docking demo from the lunar-surface mission; align with commercial HLS readiness.

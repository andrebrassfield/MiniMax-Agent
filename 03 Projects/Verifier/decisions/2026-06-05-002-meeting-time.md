# Decision 2026-06-05-002 — FDA 503A Peptide Dossier, Meeting-Time Inconsistency

> Adjudicated under Mode 3 (ADJUDICATE) of the Verifier AGENTS.md.

> Audit run: `vrd-fda-503a-2026-06-05-001`.

## Original contradiction (as flagged in the Researcher handoff)

The Researcher's inaugural FDA 503A peptide dossier surfaced a meeting-time inconsistency:

- The Federal Register notice 91 FR 20465 (Doc. 2026-07361) DATES section states verbatim: *"The meeting will be held on July 23, 2026, from 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Eastern Time and July 24, 2026, from 8:00 a.m. to 3:50 p.m. Eastern Time."*
- The FDA Advisory Committee Calendar page for the July 23-24, 2026 PCAC meeting shows top-line event time: **"8:00 a.m. - 3:50 p.m. ET"** (day-unspecific, applies to the entire event).

The Researcher best-inferred that the Federal Register per-day times are authoritative (July 23: 8am-4:30pm ET; July 24: 8am-3:50pm ET), and the FDA meeting page top-line 3:50pm is a UI simplification, at weight 0.99.

## Re-check with full source trail

The Verifier independently re-fetched both pages on 2026-06-05 ~10:25 CT. Three pieces of corroborating evidence:

1. **FR notice DATES section (verbatim):** "The meeting will be held on July 23, 2026, from 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Eastern Time and July 24, 2026, from 8:00 a.m. to 3:50 p.m. Eastern Time."

2. **FR notice per-day public-hearing session times (verbatim):** "The sessions will begin at approximately 10:15 a.m., 11:50 a.m., 2:15 p.m., and 4:00 p.m. on July 23, 2026 Eastern Time. The sessions will begin at approximately 10:20 a.m., 12:30 p.m., and 3:20 p.m. on July 24, 2026 Eastern Time." — 4 sessions on July 23 (last one at 4:00pm, consistent with a 4:30pm meeting close); 3 sessions on July 24 (last one at 3:20pm, consistent with a 3:50pm meeting close).

3. **FDA meeting page per-day oral session times (verbatim):** "Oral presentations from the public will begin at approximately 10:15 a.m., 11:50 a.m., 2:15 p.m., and 4:00 p.m. Eastern Time on July 23, 2026. Oral presentations from the public will begin at approximately 10:20 a.m., 12:30 p.m., and 3:20 p.m. Eastern Time on July 24, 2026." — matches FR per-day session times exactly. **The FDA meeting page body itself corroborates the FR per-day specificity** (4 sessions on July 23, 3 on July 24) even though its top-line event time is day-unspecific.

The 3:50pm in the FDA meeting page top-line matches July 24's FR end time. This is consistent with a UI simplification: the page uses the earlier day's end time (3:50pm) as the default event-time display, or the page is showing the day that ends earliest as a single-field summary. Either way, the page's own body (the per-day oral session schedule) confirms the per-day specificity.

## Adjudication

The Verifier **upholds the Researcher's inference** that the **Federal Register per-day times are authoritative**, and the **FDA meeting page top-line 8:00am-3:50pm ET is a UI simplification** (not a correction to the FR).

Reasoning:

1. **The Federal Register is the legal authority.** Per federal administrative law, the FR DATES section is the binding public-notice of meeting time. A top-line "Time" field on an FDA web page does not have independent legal authority to override the FR.
2. **The FDA meeting page body itself corroborates the FR per-day specificity** via the per-day oral session schedule (4 sessions on July 23 with last session at 4:00pm; 3 sessions on July 24 with last session at 3:20pm). This is internal consistency within the FDA meeting page, not a contradiction.
3. **The 3:50pm in the FDA meeting page top-line matches July 24's FR end time.** A UI that uses the day that ends earliest as a single-field summary is a common CMS pattern. Alternatively, the page may have been content-current as of 2026-04-15 (before the FR was published on 2026-04-16), and the per-day times had not been finalized.
4. **No plausible alternative explanation.** If the FDA meeting page's 3:50pm were actually a correction, it would not be day-unspecific — it would specify which day closes at 3:50pm. The day-unspecific top-line is structurally a simplification.

**Final weight on FR per-day times (July 23: 8am-4:30pm ET; July 24: 8am-3:50pm ET): 0.99.**
**Final weight on FDA meeting page top-line 8am-3:50pm ET (as the authoritative meeting time): 0.0** — superseded by the FR per-day specificity.
**Final weight on FDA meeting page top-line 8am-3:50pm ET (as a UI simplification that happens to be correct for July 24 but not for July 23):** descriptive only, not a source of authority.

The dossier should keep the FR per-day times as the authoritative meeting times. The Verifier's recommended annotation in the dossier: note that the FDA meeting page top-line 8am-3:50pm ET is a UI simplification, and the per-day specificity is what actually matters. The Scribe's downstream consumption should preserve the per-day specificity so a reader knows the July 23 meeting is a longer day than July 24.
